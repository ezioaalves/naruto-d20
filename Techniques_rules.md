APPENDIX B – CREATING YOUR OWN CONTENT
Homebrewing is the process of creating your own
unique material for use in a campaign or made to chal-
lenge yourself, help others, etc.

CREATING YOUR OWN TECHNIQUES
Many accomplished ninja have created their own tech-
niques during their adventures. While this is certainly
not necessary to become a powerful and recognised
individual, many shinobi will find it useful, as you can
create techniques that are more specific to your own
style of combat, and such techniques may help fill emp-
ty spaces in your repertoire.
Note: All of the processes and notes in this appendix
are merely a rough indicator of power levels. They are
not to be used as ‘proof’ that a self-made technique is
balanced, merely to be used as guidelines.

LEARN AND PERFORM REQUIREMENTS
All techniques require a number of statistics before
they can be used. Some of these can be calculated very
easily, such as the following:

Learn DC: 10 + Technique Rank + Modifiers
Successes: Complexity Class + Modifiers.
Skill Threshold: Technique Rank + Modifiers.
Perform DC: 10 + Technique Rank + Modifiers.
Table B-1: Technique Statistics shows the possible
modifiers and values.

A Combination technique is defined as a technique
that essentially combines two existing techniques, usu-
ally for a greater overall effect.
DEFINING RANK AND COMPLEXITY
Defining a technique’s Rank can be a challenge, and
there is no easy formula to properly categorize every
possible technique effect. However, for techniques with
common effects, an easy method is to look at existing
techniques and see which techniques deal similar dam-
age with a similar range and/or area of effect, and move
from there.
For most non-damaging techniques, the comparison
has to come from techniques that have similar effects,
such as applying conditions or penalties.
Complexity is mostly based on lore considerations:
was the technique designed for academy students, or is
it mostly wielded by Jounin-level characters? Rank and
Complexity offers some basic explanation of the differ-
ent Complexities. However, Complexity does affect a
technique’s Learn DC, Successes and Perform Require-
ments, so balance considerations are not unimportant:
Complexity is a useful guideline towards establishing a
techniques Range, Area of Effect and maximum possi-
ble effect (with higher complexities reaching greater
areas and higher possible limits). If in doubt, balance
should always be the most important.

TIME
Most techniques require an Attack action to perform.
Techniques with exceptionally powerful or long-
lasting effects, as well as most utility effects with great-
er impact (like creating structures) would require a full
round. Stances always take a Swift action.
Complex or high-level sealing techniques that use
rituals often require longer to perform, as do medical
techniques for complex methods of treatments. Such
times can range from minutes to hours, in very rare
cases even a day or more.

COMPONENTS
In the majority of cases, non-Taijutsu techniques will
require hand seals and concentration, while Taijutsu
techniques often require mobility or being physically
able, as well as a material focus if it’s a weapon attack.
Techniques will require a material focus or expenda-
ble components if their effects should logically require
them (an effect creating a whirlpool should require a
significant amount of water, raising a slab of rock
should require hard earth or rock, etc).

VITAL STATISTICS
A technique’s ‘vital statistics’ are its Range, Target(s),
Area, Damage (if any) and Cost, which are linked.
The average damage to Chakra ratio (as in, how much
average damage is dealt per point of Chakra spent) of a
technique should range from 2 to 3, based on its Rank
and Complexity (a higher Rank, and especially a higher
complexity, should allow for a higher ratio).
The average assumes a single target is hit and does
not successfully save. Attacks enhancing your melee
attacks assume one to two successful hits per round
(higher Ranks will lean towards 2). Techniques requir-
ing multiple attacks assume half the attacks hit (round-
ed up). Touch attacks are assumed to always hit. If the
technique causes a creature to catch on fire, add 1 aver-
age damage times the maximum duration of the burn
(1d4 rounds comes to an average 4 damage).

Once you’ve calculated the rough Cost the technique
should have, add increases based on its other statistics,
as shown in the table above. If your area is unique, try
to figure out what type of area would be similar in size
(if the shape allows for an easier way to target multiple

wish to add an extra +1 if the technique is not a high
Rank and/or complexity).
It should be noted that techniques of Rank 1 and 2
usually do not have Medium or Long range, and that
techniques below Rank 6 are usually limited to Medi-
um range at best. Similarly, techniques Rank 2 or lower
usually don’t have an area of effect, and techniques
Rank 8 or lower usually don’t have an area that would
grant it a +2 bonus to the cost.
TAIJUTSU TECHNIQUES
Generally speaking, Taijutsu techniques cost slightly
less, as such techniques have short range, rely on attack
rolls and deal types of damage that are most likely to be
resisted.
EMPOWERMENT
In most cases, a technique offering empowerment ei-
ther adds 1d6 damage per point of Chakra. If the tech-
nique deals damage in higher die sizes, the cost should
be higher (such as 2 per 1d10, or 3 per 2d8).
If the technique’s empowerment merely allows firing
multiple projectiles (or affecting multiple targets/areas
with the same effect), the cost of empowering should
equal the base cost of the technique (1 more or less is
allowed based on the complexity and Rank).
NON-DAMAGING EFFECTS
A technique that adds a minor effect (such as a
knockback, a slight penalty to certain rolls, small tem-
porary ability score penalty, deafness, and effects of a
similar impact) usually adds +1 to its Cost, or +2 if it
affects an area.
If the effect is major (stun, paralysis), the cost in-
creases by 2, or 3 for techniques with a small area of
effect. Such effects should generally not be added to
techniques with larger areas of effect. The duration of
such effects should also be minor (1 round for areas of
effect, up to 2 or 3 average rounds for techniques with a
single target).
Chakra Coils damage is very rare, and should be used
very sparingly. Usually, such techniques deal subpar
damage for their cost and rank, and always directly
target individual creatures (never areas of effect).
The duration of the effect should scale based on Rank
and complexity: A rank 3 technique should not be ca-
pable of stunning creatures for 2 or 3 rounds.
TECHNIQUES DEALING NO DAMAGE
Techniques whose primary use is utility have no set
rules: it is best to compare their level of usefulness to
existing utility techniques.
Techniques that primarily afflict targets with condi-
tions or penalties should try to compare them to exist-
ing techniques, as the possibilities vary greatly and such
effects are often difficult to quantify.

ENHANCEMENT SEALS AND SEALED ITEMS
More than anything, enhancement seals and sealed
items rely on comparative strength: there is no tried
and true method for assigning a power level other than
trying to compare them to existing enhancement seals
or sealed items. However, a few metrics do have steady
rules that you can follow:

SEAL LEVEL AND RANK
A seal’s level and rank are linked: If you know what
Rank a seal has, you can determine the range of levels it
is meant for. Similarly, if you know its intended level,
you will know its Rank. Table B-2: Special Tool Costs
shows these relations.
If the item carries an enhancement bonus, it should
not exceed +1 per rank (i.e. Greater is no more than
+3). If the additional effects on the seal are minor, you
can simply raise its level within the rank boundaries. If
the effects are substantial, you may need to increase the
seal’s level and Rank more.
