# Wild Technician

The wild technician is a master of the unpredictable, so skilled he continuously surprises even himself. To the uninitiated, a wild technician only has a tenuous mastery of his craft, but a skilled observer can recognize the signs for what they are: of a dangerous adept whose wrath is a simmering cauldron waiting to be unleashed upon anyone unlucky enough to stand between the wild technician and his objective.

## Requirements

To qualify to become a wild technician, a character must fulfill all the following criteria.
*   **Ability Score**: Constitution 13.
*   **Skills**: Any of the following skills at 6 ranks or more: Chakra Control, Fuinjutsu, Genjutsu or Ninjutsu.
*   **Feats**: Improved Chakra Pool and any meta-chakra feat.

## Class Information

The following information pertains to the Wild Technician advanced class.

*   **Hit Die**: The Wild Technician gains 1d8 hit points per level. The Constitution modifier applies.
*   **Action Points**: The Wild Technician gains a number of action points equal to 6 + one-half his character level, rounded down, everytime he attains a new level in this class.
*   **Class Skills**: The Wild Technician's class skills are as follows: Bluff (Cha), Computer Use (Int), Decipher Script (Int), Disable Device (Int), Drive (Dex), Fuinjutsu (Int), Genjutsu (Cha), Handle Animal (Cha), Intimidate (Cha), Investigate (Int), Listen (Wis), Knowledge (arcane lore, history, ninja lore, theology and philosophy) (Int), Ninjutsu (Int), Profession (Wis), Read/Write Language (None), Sign Language (None), Speak Language (None), Spot (Wis).
*   **Skill Points at Each Level**: 7 + Int modifier.

## Class Features

The following features pertain to the Wild Technician advanced class.

### Null Entity
Becoming a wild technician is a transformative experience. At 1st level, the wild technician's maximum chakra reserve becomes 0, effectively losing the ability to gain chakra reserves by any means, as well as the penalties for having a low reserve. In addition, the wild technician gains a secondary chakra pool called null chakra, which is equal to one-half the wild technician's chakra pool. The null chakra pool is reset to 0 at the end of a night of rest, and cannot be drawn from to perform techniques. Likewise, a null chakra pool of 0 does not incur chakra depletion.

Any time the wild technician uses a Chakra Control, Fuinjutsu, Genjutsu or Ninjutsu technique, he adds the technique's base cost to his null chakra pool. When the wild technician's null chakra pool is full, he enters a state of wild chakra until it is empty again.

In wild chakra, any chakra cost is deducted from the wild technician's null chakra pool instead of his normal chakra pool, and does not further increase null chakra pool until the wild chakra state ends. Furthermore, every technique performed must be empowered to the maximum extent the character is capable of and provokes a wild surge (see below). When the wild chakra pool is too low to pay for the full cost of a technique, that technique must still be empowered to its fullest extent, and the remainder of the cost is drawn from the wild technician's normal chakra pool.

### Wild Surge
When a wild technician provokes a wild surge, it threatens to create an effect that is entirely outside his control. However, it is possible to try and contain the energy of the surge by making a Constitution check (DC 15) with a bonus equal to one-half his wild technician class levels (round up). Whether the surge is contained or not, the GM secretly rolls 1d20 and consults the appropriate column on the table below. The effect happens at the end of the wild technician's turn.

### Boundless Chakra
Though the wild technician has no chakra reserves to speak of, his chakra pool is the stuff of legend. At 1st level and every wild technician level afterwards, his chakra pool increases by 4. This affects the size of his null chakra pool as well.

### Wild Technique
At 2nd level, a wild technician learns to perform a wild technique. This technique provokes a wild surge, but increases the effective character level the technique is performed at by +1 at 2nd level, +2 at 5th level and +3 at 8th level. If the wild technician uses a meta-chakra feat on a wild technique, the perform requirements are not increased and the increased chakra cost is also added to the wild technician's null chakra pool (if any).

The wild technician can use this ability once per day every two wild technician levels.

### Bonus Feat
At 3rd, 6th and 9th level, the Wild Technician gets a bonus feat. The bonus feat must be a meta-chakra feat, and the Wild Technician must meet all the prerequisites of the feat to select it.

### Delay Surge
At 4th level, the wild technician gains a measure of control over his wild chakra. Whenever he provokes a wild surge, the wild technician can use this ability to delay its effects by one round every 2 wild technician levels he has. He can use this ability once per day, then an additional time per day at 7th and 10th level.

### Purgative Transference
At 7th level, the wild technician can spend a meta-chakra charge and touch a creature to transfer some of their chakra to them. The wild technician chooses how much chakra is transferred, and that amount is taken from his null chakra pool. The target gains that same amount as temporary chakra for 1 hour, and incurs a wild surge the next time it spends any chakra. The wild technician can transfer no more than his wild technician level in chakra at a time. Any use of the ability after the first deducts more and more chakra (twice as much for the second use, three times for the third, etc.).

### Destructive Surge
At 10th level, the wild technician has mastered the ability to summon his wild chakra on command. Whenever the wild technician uses the Wild Technique ability, the wild surge is always contained unless the wild technician declares otherwise. In addition, if a wild technique can be empowered to deal damage to a maximum number of dice limited, the number of dice it can be empowered to increases by an amount equal to the wild technician's Wild Technique bonus.

When performing a wild technique, the wild technician can spend an action point to apply the effects of a meta-chakra feat to a technique that has already been enhanced by another meta-chakra feat, even when he otherwise couldn't do so. The wild technician can use this effect without spending an action point once per day.
