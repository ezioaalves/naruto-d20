# Outlaw

The outlaw is someone who makes his own rules. He takes what he wants when he sees it, without a care for whom or what stands in his way. He is a thug who can make good use of his environment to scare or assault and put a swift end to anyone with the courage to stand up to him.

## Requirements

To qualify to become an outlaw, a character must fulfill all the following criteria.
*   **Base Attack Bonus**: +3.
*   **Skills**: Hide 3 ranks, Intimidate 5 ranks, Move Silently 3 ranks, Sleight of Hand 8 ranks.
*   **Feats**: Nimble.

## Class Information

The following information pertains to the Outlaw prestige class.

*   **Hit Die**: The Outlaw gains 1d6 hit points per level. The Constitution modifier applies.
*   **Action Points**: The Outlaw gains a number of action points equal to 7 + one-half his character level, rounded down, everytime he attains a new level in this class.
*   **Class Skills**: The Outlaw's class skills are as follows: Balance (Dex), Bluff (Cha), Climb (Str), Diplomacy (Cha), Disable Device (Int), Drive (Dex), Escape Artist (Dex), Gamble (Wis), Gather Information (Cha), Hide (Dex), Intimidate (Cha), Jump (Str), Knowledge (current events, popular culture, streetwise, tactics) (Int), Listen (Wis), Move Silently (Dex), Navigate (Int), Profession (Wis), Search (Int), Sense Motive (Wis), Sleight of Hand (Dex), Spot (Wis), Survival (Wis), and Tumble (Dex).
*   **Skill Points at Each Level**: 7 + Int modifier.

## Class Features

The following features pertain to the Outlaw prestige class.

### Prey Spotting
At 1st level, the outlaw is already adept at spotting and following his mark. He gains a +2 competence bonus to Hide checks to tail a person in public and to Survival checks in urban areas.

### Improvised Weapons
At 2nd level, the outlaw is adept at fighting with whatever he has on hand and no longer suffers a –4 penalty to attack rolls when wielding an improvised weapon.

### Sneak Attack
This ability functions as the Ninja Scout sneak attack ability. If the outlaw can catch an opponent when he is unable to defend himself effectively from his attack, he can strike a vital spot for extra damage. Creatures immune to critical hits and sneak attacks, or who have concealment, do not suffer extra damage.

This extra damage is 1d6 at 3rd level, and it increases to 2d6 at 5th level. If the outlaw gains sneak attack damage from another source, they stack.

### Bully
At 3rd level, the outlaw adds his Reputation bonus as a circumstance bonus to Intimidate checks, up to his outlaw level. In addition, the outlaw gains a +2 bonus to Charisma-based checks and Sleight of Hand checks to pickpocket an intimidated target.

### Improvised Weapons Damage
At 4th level, the outlaw deals more damage with improvised weapons and treats all improvised weapons as though one size category larger than it is to determine the damage it deals.

### Excessive Force
At 5th level, the outlaw gains the ability to rob an opponent in the middle of combat. As an attack action, the outlaw can spend an action point to make a melee attack unarmed or with any light or one-handed weapon. After the attack, the outlaw can immediately make a Sleight of Hand check to take an object from the opponent without provoking an attack of opportunity (the opponent may make a Spot check at a –5 penalty opposed to the outlaw's Sleight of Hand check to notice the attempt).

### Bonus Feat
At 2nd and 4th level, the Outlaw gets a bonus feat. The bonus feat must be selected from the following list, and the Outlaw must meet all the prerequisites of the feat to select it.
* [Universal: Defensive Stance]
* Confident
* Deceptive
* Powerful Maneuvers
* Deft Maneuvers
* [Universal: Risky Strike]
